Research directions

We bring together advanced microwave techniques and designer 2D materials to probe quantum matter in ways traditional DC transport cannot.

01 · Programmed phases

Programmable quantum phases in 2D materials

We use gates, strain, and engineered geometries to create and control correlated and topological states in 2D materials — aiming for “phase FPGAs” that go beyond moiré and allow flexible exploration of quantum phase diagrams.

02 · GHz transport

Microwave quantum transport

We extend quantum transport into the GHz regime to access dynamics, collective modes, and dissipation that are invisible in DC measurements, from quantum Hall edges and fractional Chern insulators to unconventional superconductivity.

03 · Imaging

Microwave quantum imaging & scanning probes

We develop microwave and millimeter-wave scanning probes that map local phases, domains, and edges with nanoscale resolution, providing a “microwave view” of inhomogeneity and emergent order in quantum materials.
