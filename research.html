Research

Microwave eyes on quantum matter

We work at the intersection of microwave techniques and quantum materials, asking what new phases, modes, and organizing principles appear when you look at 2D systems in the GHz regime and in real space.

Our long-term goal is to build a kind of “phase FPGA” for 2D materials – where gates, strain, geometry, and microwaves let us program and read out correlated and topological phases on demand.

Main directions

Students and postdocs are encouraged to work across thrusts and to help invent the next generation of tools and questions.

01 · Programmed phases

Programmable quantum phases in 2D materials

We treat 2D materials as platforms where phases can be written and rewritten. Using local gates, engineered edges, strain, and tailored device geometries, we explore how to stabilize and manipulate correlated and topological states – including fractional Chern insulators, unconventional superconductors, and potentially anyonic excitations – without relying only on a specific moiré angle.

For students: This thrust combines device design, nanofabrication, and conceptual thinking about phase diagrams and effective models.

02 · GHz transport

Microwave quantum transport

Traditional transport focuses on DC or low-frequency response. We extend transport into the GHz regime to probe dynamics and collective modes that are invisible in DC measurements: edge magnetoplasmons, chiral and helical edge channels, superconducting phase dynamics, and other emergent excitations.

For students: You will design and measure microwave circuits in dilution refrigerators, interpret complex spectra, and connect them to real many-body physics.

03 · Imaging

Microwave imaging & scanning probes

Real materials are not perfectly homogeneous. We develop microwave and millimeter-wave scanning probes that can map out local phases, domains, and edges with high spatial resolution – giving a “microwave view” of inhomogeneity, domain structure, and emergent order in 2D materials and devices.

For students: This thrust is ideal if you love instrumentation: building and aligning microscopes, designing tips and resonators, and transforming raw signals into images of quantum structure.

How you might fit into this picture

We welcome people with different backgrounds – transport, AMO-style thinking, nanofabrication, microwaves, or theory – who want to learn from each other.

  • If you love building things, you might lead the development of a new cryogenic microwave setup or scanning probe.
  • If you love data and theory, you might help translate complex frequency- and space-resolved signals into physical stories and simple models.
  • If you love materials and devices, you might design the next generation of programmable 2D structures that these tools will probe.
  • Many of the best ideas will come from the boundaries between these roles. We are excited to learn from you as much as you learn from the lab.
