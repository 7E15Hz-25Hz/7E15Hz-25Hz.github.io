Research

Microwave eyes on quantum matter

Our research combines microwave quantum transport, microwave scanning probes, and programmable 2D materials to explore correlated and topological phases beyond what DC measurements can see.

Research directions

Below are our main thrusts. Each combines new instruments with new questions about how quantum phases form, move, and interact.

01 · Programmed phases

Programmable quantum phases in 2D materials

We use gates, strain, and engineered geometries to create and control correlated and topological states in 2D materials — aiming for “phase FPGAs” that go beyond moiré and allow flexible exploration of quantum phase diagrams, including anyonic and fractionalized excitations.

02 · GHz transport

Microwave quantum transport

We extend transport into the GHz regime to probe dynamics and collective modes that are invisible in DC measurements. This includes microwave signatures of quantum Hall edges, fractional Chern insulators, unconventional superconductivity, and other strongly correlated phases.

03 · Imaging

Microwave imaging & scanning probes

We develop microwave and millimeter-wave scanning probes that resolve local phases, domains, and edges with high spatial resolution. These tools give a “microwave view” of inhomogeneity, domain structure, and emergent order in quantum materials and devices.
